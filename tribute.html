Vincent Van Gogh

The man who took great pain and used it to create ecstatic beauty.

Vincent Van Gogh was a Dutch post-impressionist painter who lived in the 19th century. He is known for his colorful and expressive artworks, which often depict landscapes, still life, and portraits. Despite struggling with mental illness throughout his life, Van Gogh created over 2,000 works of art in just a decade, including some of the most recognizable paintings in the world, such as "The Starry Night" and "Sunflowers." His unique style of using bold brushstrokes and vivid colors had a significant impact on the development of modern art. Although he was not recognized during his lifetime, Van Gogh's legacy continues to influence artists and inspire people around the world.

The Life of Van Gogh
  • 30 March 1853: Van Gogh is born in Groot-Zundert, the Netherlands.
  • 1857: Vincent's brother, Theo, is born. The two remained close all Vincent's life.
  • 1861-68: Vincent attends school in the Netherlands. During his schooling he displayed no signs of unique artistic ability. He would not show promise until the late 1880s.
  • July 1869: Vincent lands a job in Hague at Goupil & Cie, an art dealership with various galleries dotted around Europe.
  • June 1873: Vincent is transferred to Goupil's London branch. He lodges with a landlady and her daughter, whom he'd fall into unrequitted love with, in south London.
  • 1876-80: Vincent leaves Goupil & Cie by mutual consent. Returning home, he turned to religion and became a lay preacher in Belgium.
    • \
  • 1880: Vincent moves to Brussels, with Theo's financial support, to study art.
  • 1881: Another in a string on unsuccessful relationships, Vincent falls in love with his cousin, Kee Vos-Stricker. She rejects his advances. Vincent responds by following her to her parents and demanding to see her, burning himself with a candle when refused by her parents.
